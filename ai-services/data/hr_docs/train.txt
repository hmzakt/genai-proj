1. Introduction

This document outlines the Human Resources (HR) policies of the company. These policies are designed to ensure fairness, transparency, and consistency in the treatment of all employees. All employees are expected to read, understand, and comply with the policies described in this handbook. The company reserves the right to modify, update, or discontinue any policy at its discretion.

2. Employment Types

The company employs individuals under the following categories:

Full-Time Employees: Employees working 40 hours per week on a permanent basis.

Part-Time Employees: Employees working fewer than 40 hours per week.

Contract Employees: Employees hired for a fixed duration or specific project.

Interns: Temporary employees hired for learning and training purposes.

Benefits and entitlements may vary depending on employment type.

3. Working Hours and Attendance
3.1 Working Hours

Standard working hours are 9:30 AM to 6:30 PM, Monday to Friday.

Employees are expected to be available during core hours from 11:00 AM to 4:00 PM.

3.2 Attendance

Employees must mark attendance daily using the companyâ€™s attendance system.

Late arrivals or early departures should be informed to the reporting manager in advance.

3.3 Overtime

Overtime is applicable only with prior managerial approval.

Overtime compensation may be provided as compensatory off or monetary compensation, depending on company policy.

4. Leave Policy
4.1 Types of Leave
a) Casual Leave (CL)

Employees are entitled to 12 casual leaves per year.

Casual leave can be taken for personal reasons or emergencies.

Unused casual leave does not carry forward.

b) Sick Leave (SL)

Employees are entitled to 10 sick leaves per year.

Sick leave may be taken for illness or medical emergencies.

A medical certificate may be required for sick leave exceeding 2 consecutive days.

c) Earned / Privilege Leave (PL)

Employees earn 1.5 days of earned leave per month.

Earned leave can be accumulated up to a maximum of 30 days.

Earned leave can be encashed at the time of resignation, subject to company policy.

d) Maternity Leave

Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave, as per applicable law.

Maternity leave must be applied at least 8 weeks in advance.

e) Paternity Leave

Male employees are entitled to 5 working days of paternity leave.

Paternity leave must be taken within 30 days of childbirth.

4.2 Leave Application Process

All leaves must be applied through the HR portal.

Leave approval is subject to manager approval.

In case of emergency, verbal or email approval is acceptable but must be regularized later.

5. Holidays

The company observes national and regional holidays as per the annual holiday calendar.

The holiday list is published at the beginning of each calendar year.

Employees may also be eligible for restricted holidays, which can be chosen from a predefined list.

6. Payroll and Compensation
6.1 Salary Structure

Salary is paid on a monthly basis, usually on or before the 5th working day of each month.

Salary components may include:

Basic Pay

House Rent Allowance (HRA)

Special Allowance

Performance Bonus (if applicable)

6.1.1 Bonus Calculation
Performance bonuses are calculated annually based on the employee's performance rating:
- Rating 5 (Outstanding): 20% of annual Basic Pay
- Rating 4 (Exceeds Expectations): 15% of annual Basic Pay
- Rating 3 (Meets Expectations): 10% of annual Basic Pay
- Rating 1-2: No bonus

The bonus is disbursed in the April payroll.

6.2 Deductions

Statutory deductions include:

Provident Fund (PF)

Professional Tax (PT)

Income Tax (TDS)

Other deductions may include loan repayments or recovery of advances.

6.3 Payslips

Payslips are available on the HR portal.

Employees are advised to review payslips monthly and report discrepancies to HR within 7 days.

7. Performance Management
7.1 Performance Reviews

Performance reviews are conducted bi-annually.

Employees are evaluated based on:

Goal achievement

Skill development

Team collaboration

Professional behavior

7.2 Promotions and Appraisals

Promotions and salary revisions are merit-based.

Final decisions are taken by management based on performance, company growth, and budget.

8. Code of Conduct

Employees are expected to:

Maintain professionalism and integrity at all times.

Treat colleagues, clients, and stakeholders with respect.

Avoid conflicts of interest.

Protect company assets and confidential information.

Any violation of the code of conduct may result in disciplinary action.

9. Information Security and Confidentiality

Employees must not share confidential company information with unauthorized persons.

Company systems must be used responsibly.

Data security guidelines must be strictly followed.

10. Remote Work and Hybrid Policy

Remote work is subject to managerial approval.

Employees must ensure availability during working hours.

The company reserves the right to revoke remote work privileges if productivity is impacted.

11. Grievance Redressal

Employees may raise grievances related to work, harassment, or policy issues.

Grievances can be reported to:

Reporting Manager

HR Department

All grievances will be handled confidentially and fairly.

12. Resignation and Notice Period
12.1 Notice Period

The standard notice period is 30 days for full-time employees.

Notice period may vary based on role or contract.

12.2 Exit Process

Employees must complete knowledge transfer and handover.

Company assets must be returned before the last working day.

Final settlement is processed within 30 days of the last working day.

13. Termination

The company reserves the right to terminate employment in cases of:

Gross misconduct

Policy violations

Poor performance (after due process)

Termination will be carried out in accordance with applicable laws.

14. Amendments and Policy Updates

The company may amend policies at any time.

Employees will be notified of major changes via official communication channels.
